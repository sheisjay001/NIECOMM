# User Onboarding Flow --- UI/UX README

This document describes a complete, fun, and conversion-focused user
onboarding flow.\
It covers **UX philosophy, UI design system, screen-by-screen copy, and
interaction principles**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose of This Onboarding

Onboarding is the user's first relationship with the product.\
This flow is designed to:

-   Reduce friction
-   Build trust quickly
-   Personalize the experience
-   Feel human, playful, and respectful of time

Target completion time: **under 60 seconds**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Design Principles

1.  **Visible progress** --- Users always know where they are.
2.  **Low pressure choices** --- No "required" anxiety.
3.  **Human language** --- Short, warm, confident copy.

Think: friendly guide, not bureaucratic gatekeeper.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors** - Midnight Blue: `#0E1B2A` (Trust, stability) -
Electric Cyan: `#3DF2D0` (Energy, delight)

**Secondary Colors** - Soft Lavender: `#A78BFA` (Creativity) - Sun
Coral: `#FF7A59` (Calls-to-action)

**Backgrounds** - Cloud White: `#F8FAFC` - Mist Gray: `#E5E7EB`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Typography

-   Headlines: Inter / Satoshi / SF Pro (Semi-Bold)
-   Body Text: Inter (Regular)
-   Buttons: Inter (Medium)

Use sentence case. Avoid all caps.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### UI Style Guidelines

-   Rounded corners (12--16px)
-   Soft shadows, no harsh borders
-   Subtle animations
-   Thin progress bar at the top

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Onboarding Flow Structure

Total screens: **5**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Screen 1 --- Welcome

**Goal:** Emotional warmth and curiosity

**Copy** \> Welcome to \[App Name\]\
\> Let's set things up so this feels like your space.

Primary CTA:\
**Let's go**

Helper text:\
*Takes less than a minute.*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Screen 2 --- Identity Selection

**Goal:** Light personalization

**Copy** \> What best describes you?\
\> Pick anything that fits. You can change this later.

Options: - Student - Professional - Founder - Creator - Just exploring

CTA: **Continue**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Screen 3 --- User Intent

**Goal:** Align product value with motivation

**Copy** \> What do you want to do first?

Options: - Grow something - Learn something new - Automate my work -
Communicate better - Just look around

Helper text: *This helps us tailor your experience.*

CTA: **Next**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Screen 4 --- Personal Touch

**Goal:** Create emotional connection

**Copy** \> What should we call you?

Placeholder: *Your name or nickname*

Helper text: *Just for a more personal experience.*

CTA: **Almost done**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Micro-Interactions

-   Button tap: scale to 98%
-   Smooth progress bar animation
-   Gentle confetti on success
-   Friendly error messages

Example error: \> That didn't work. Let's try again.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Accessibility Considerations

-   Minimum text size: 14px
-   AA contrast compliance
-   Haptic feedback on mobile
-   No forced permissions during onboarding

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Why This Works

This onboarding flow:

-   Reduces cognitive load
-   Creates emotional safety
-   Makes the product feel adaptive
-   Encourages completion without force

Onboarding is not setup.\
It is the first promise your product makes.
